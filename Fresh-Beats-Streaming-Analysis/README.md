## Executive Summary
A comprehensive performance analysis evaluating user behavior across multi-year streaming cycles to uncover the true drivers of subscription growth and listener retention. This project goes beyond surface metrics like total user counts and instead evaluates tier-specific engagement, genre lifecycle shifts, and track-to-user ratios to determine what actually sustains high platform performance in a competitive digital landscape.

---

## Project Overview
Using Google Sheets and advanced Pivot Table modeling, this analysis investigates how platform growth is influenced by engagement behaviors rather than raw popularity. By comparing legacy popularity signals (total user volume) with modern quality indicators (tracks per user and subscription tier activity), the project establishes a scalable framework for identifying high-performing genres and high-value listener segments.

---

## Business Problem
Traditional streaming KPIs fail to reflect real listener quality. Relying on total user volume creates several blind spots:
* **The Metric Paradox** — Total user counts often reflect historical trends, not current engagement levels.
* **Engagement Gaps** — Difficulty in distinguishing "casual" listeners from high-value power users.
* **Feedback Noise** — Generic volume metrics drown out meaningful behavior-based insights.
* **Engagement Volatility** — No visibility into how subscription tier benefits stabilize user activity over time.

---

## Solution
I engineered a diagnostic analysis built around engagement-centric metrics that redefine what “quality” means for a streaming service. The system includes:
* **Tier Segmentation** — Isolates behavior between Free and Paid users to identify the "Engagement Gap."
* **Retention Tracking** — "Tracks Per User" metrics to quantify listener loyalty and platform utility.
* **Lifecycle Mapping** — Tracks 5-year trends to identify declining vs. emerging genres.
* **Satisfaction Correlation** — Proves that high-engagement genres (like Indie and Rock) maintain more stable long-term growth.

## Workflow Breakdown
1. **Data Aggregation**: Analyzed 5 years of listener data across multiple performance dimensions.
2. **Quality Normalization**: Applied "Tracks Per User" formulas to focus on meaningful engagement levels.
3. **Trend Mapping**: Ranked genres by volume of tracks and year-over-year user growth.
4. **Impact Analysis**: Visualized relationships between subscription status, genre loyalty, and long-term retention.

---

## Key Outcomes
* **Identified High-Performing Segments** — Rock and Indie stand out as the most stable and loyal user bases.
* **Revenue Optimization** — Identified a high-value conversion opportunity within the high-volume Free Rock segment.
* **Redefined Success Metrics** — Demonstrated that total user volume is a weaker indicator of growth than engagement-driven indicators.
* **Retention Insights** — Clear correlation: genres with high engagement ratios provide the most stable platform growth.

---

## Why This Project Matters
This analysis shows how modern BI can uncover hidden streaming dynamics that traditional KPIs overlook. Focusing on engagement ratios, genre stability, and tier-specific behavior enables better decision-making, higher user satisfaction, and scalable revenue quality.
